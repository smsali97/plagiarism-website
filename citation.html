Citation is the practice of mentioning sources/refrences in your work. Citation may mention books,articles,etc..

Why is citation needed?

A major motive of a citation is logical sincerity to point prior or copied work and facts to the exact sources, and to permit the reader to conclude autonomously whether the situation matter sustains the author's dispute in the claimed method.

 

Citation Content:

Citation content can vary depending on the type of source and may include:

  • Book: author(s), book heading, publisher, date of publication, and page number if suitable.

  • Journal: author(s), article heading, journal name, date of publication, and page number.

  • Newspaper: author(s), article label, name of newspaper, segment title and page number(s) if required, date of publication.



  • Web site: author(s), article and publication name where fitting, in addition a URL, and a date when the site was referred to.


  • Poem: spaced slashes are usually used to point out different lines of a poem, and parenthetical citations usually include the line number(s).
