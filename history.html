THE HISTORY OF PLAGIARISM

 

It all started with poetry and Romans...

No really it did, believe it or not, plagiarism is as old as the 1st Century. In the 1st Century A Roman poet named Martial had found another poet "kidnapping his verses" . At that time this plagiarism was referred as as "plagiarius" as in literal kidnapper. I know, right?

Then, in the 1600s a dramatist Ben Johnson introduced this word in English, describing it as plagiary (someone guilty of theft).

However the real form of plagiarism started in the 18th Century during the Romantic Movement in Europe. It was during this "Romantic" period, when the free will of copying/borrowing someone's work was banned. So, no sculpture, art, music couldn't be copied at free will anymore.

Mind you, this was the time of Shakespeare..so can you imagine if someone copied Shakespeare?? They would at least be a thousand Shakespeares to this day?!!!

 

Then later in 1755, Johnson's famous dictionary has introduced the word plagiary.
